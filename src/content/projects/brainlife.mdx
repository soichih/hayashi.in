[brainlife.io](https://brainlife.io) is an open-source, decentralized cloud platform that lets
neuroscientists publish datasets and analysis apps and run them through a web UI backed by
national supercomputers, private clouds, and institutional HPC.

As technical lead and senior engineer (Indiana University, 2017–2022), I designed a highly
scalable backend that handles hundreds of researchers running millions of jobs per year,
operated the production service stack and high-availability infrastructure, and built flagship
neuroimaging visualizers and GPU-enabled native UI streaming.

The platform standardizes, manages, visualizes, and processes data while automatically tracking
the provenance of thousands of data objects — making research **findable, accessible,
interoperable, and reproducible** (FAIR). It was validated across four data modalities and
3,200 participants.

- **Published:** Hayashi, S. et al. "brainlife.io: a decentralized and open-source cloud
  platform to support neuroscience research." *Nature Methods* 21, 809–813 (2024).
  [doi:10.1038/s41592-024-02237-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-024-02237-2)
